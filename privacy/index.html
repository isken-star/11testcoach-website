Privacy Policy

Last updated: 23 July 2026

11+ Test Coach is designed so your family's data stays yours. This policy explains, in plain language, what the app does — and, more importantly, what it doesn't do.

The short version

We do not collect, transmit, sell or share any personal data. There are no accounts, no cloud sync, no analytics, no advertising and no tracking of any kind. Everything the app knows lives only on your device.

What data the app stores — and where

  • Child profiles (first name, optional age, chosen settings), learning progress (answers, scores, streaks, XP, mock test results), target school selections and reward goals are all stored locally on your iPhone or iPad, inside the app's own storage.
  • None of this information ever leaves the device unless you choose to export it (see below).

What we don't do

  • No online accounts — there is nothing to sign up for and no password to create.
  • No third-party analytics, advertising SDKs or tracking technologies.
  • No transmission of your child's data to us or anyone else.
  • No in-app links that take children out of the app — external links appear only in the PIN-protected Parent Mode.

Notifications

Practice reminders and reward notifications are local notifications, scheduled and delivered entirely on the device. They require your permission via the standard iOS prompt and can be switched off at any time in Parent Mode or iOS Settings.

Backups and exports

Parent Mode lets you create backup files and PDF progress reports. These are generated on-device and are only ever shared if you choose to share them (for example by AirDrop, email or Files). They remain entirely under your control. Standard iCloud device backups made by iOS may include the app's data, governed by Apple's own privacy terms.

Children's privacy

The app is made for children preparing for the 11+, set up and supervised by a parent or guardian. Because the app collects and transmits nothing, there is no personal information about your child for us — or anyone else — to see. Anything parent-facing sits behind a PIN.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of the app ever changes how data is handled, we will update this page and the policy inside the app before that version is released, and the app will always ask for any new permissions explicitly.
